Diamond Painting Kit Contents

  • pattern sheet with glue layer covered with a protective film
  • coded rhinestones
  • pen for rhinestones
  • sorting tray
  • silicone wax
  • manual

Making a Diamond Painting

  1. Place the partern on a flat working area and straighten it out.
  2. Fill the rhinstone pen with the silicone wax, by pressing the tip on to the wax. Be sure to remove excess was from the pen for a cleaner user.
  3. Peel off the protective film off the pattern. Don't peel off the film completely to not expose the sticky surface.
  4. The pattern is coded that matches the color of the rhinestone to be placed.
  5. The tray can be used to sort and prepare the rhinestones to be used.
  6. Use the pen to lift the stones from the tray to the pattern.
  7. Place the stone accurently on the adhesive pattern.

Tips and Tricks

  • When opening the pattern sheet, it's good to let it rest with weights so it can be flattend out.
  • Organize the rhinestones into small containers. This will make it easier change from one rhinestone to another.
  • Work in small section and only peel small parts at a time. This will keep the integrity of the pattern better.
  • Use tweezers to remove wrong rhinestones on the pattern.
  • Sometimes, it can be tricky to place stones in between stones already put. Apply a bit of extra pressure to slide the stone into place.
  • When more stones are put, other stones can be pushed up, use a rolling pin to push all stone back in place and to make sure all stones are sticking well on the pattern.
